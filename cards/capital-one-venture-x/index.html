❓ Frequently asked questions

Is the Venture X really only $395/year?
Effectively, no. After the $300 travel credit and 10K anniversary miles ($100), the net AF is ~$0/yr if you book any travel through Capital One Travel. It's the cheapest premium card available.
How does the Venture X compare to Chase Sapphire Reserve and Amex Platinum?
Venture X is $150–$300 cheaper than Reserve/Platinum and includes lounge access, but transfer partners are weaker (no Hyatt, no Delta) and the travel portal can be pricier than booking direct.
Are Capital One miles worth it?
Best value at 1.85¢+ via transfer partners (Air Canada Aeroplan, Avianca LifeMiles, Turkish Airlines). At 1¢ for "purchase eraser" they're mediocre; you need to learn transfer partners.
Can I add authorized users for free?
Yes. Authorized users get full Priority Pass and Capital One Lounge access — that alone is worth ~$700/yr if your spouse travels with you.
What's the catch with the $300 travel credit?
Must book through Capital One Travel portal. Prices are sometimes 3–10% higher than booking direct, so the credit's real value is closer to $250–$280 if you'd normally book elsewhere.
Is the Venture X hard to get approved for?
Yes — Capital One is conservative. You'll typically need a 740+ FICO, low utilization, and clean recent inquiries. They also auto-decline applicants with too many recent Capital One products.
