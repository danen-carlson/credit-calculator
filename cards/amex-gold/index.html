❓ Frequently asked questions

Is the Amex Gold worth $325 annually?
If you actually use the credits ($424/yr face value), the card pays for itself before factoring rewards. The catch: most credits are split monthly/semi-annually so you have to remember to redeem them.
What's the best way to use Membership Rewards points?
Transfer partners (Delta, ANA, Air France/KLM, Hilton, Marriott) typically yield 1.5–2.5¢/point. Avoid the gift card / pay-with-points-on-Amazon redemption — those are ~0.7¢/point.
Are Amex Gold credits hard to use?
They're prorated monthly, which means you actively have to redeem them. People who set monthly Uber/Dunkin' reminders maximize value; people who don't end up leaving $200+/yr on the table.
Does the Amex Gold count as a charge card?
It's technically a charge card with Pay Over Time — most spend must be paid in full each month, but you can opt eligible charges into a revolving plan with interest.
How does the Amex Gold compare to the Chase Sapphire Preferred?
Gold is better for dining and groceries (4x vs 3x). Sapphire Preferred is cheaper ($95 vs $325) and has stronger travel insurance + transfer partners. Pick Gold if you spend $1K+/mo on restaurants/groceries.
Is the Amex Gold worth it without using credits?
Less compelling. Strip out the $424 in coupon credits and you're paying $325/yr for 4x dining. Cards like Citi Custom Cash give 5% on a chosen category for $0 AF.
